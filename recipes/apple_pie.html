Apple Pie

This is the first on the dessert table to be devoured at any function or festivity!

I made this up from a pie I had eaten at a restaurant years ago. This recipe makes two deep dish pies.

Ingredients

  • Butter:2 ounces butter
  • Brown sugar:1/2 cup packed brown sugar
  • Flour:1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • Nuts:1 cup chopped pecans
  • Apples:9 apple-pealed, cored and sliced
  • White sugar:1/4 cup white sugar
  • All-purpose flour:1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
  • Cinnamon:1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Pie shells:2 recipes unbaked pie shells

    • Steps

    1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 C).
    2. Prepare the topping by combining the butter, brown sugar, flour and chopped pecans. Cut in all ingredients with pastry cutter until pea-sized. Set aside (175 C)
    3. Combine the apples, sugar, flour and cinnamon. Toss gently together and set aside. (175 C)
    4. Place apple mixture in unbaked pie shells. Heap the apples up high in each shell and fill completely. Sprinkle topping on top of each.
    5. Bake at 350 degrees F (175 degrees C) for 50 minutes. Do not allow topping to get too dark.
